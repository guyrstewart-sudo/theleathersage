01 — The Essence

A talisman you can wear

The Leather Sage makes hand-cut, gemstone-set leather adornments for people who carry meaning on their bodies.

Every piece begins with a hide and a stone. From there it is cut, tooled, stitched, and set entirely by hand in a small Asheville studio. The result is wearable art — hip bags cradling charoite, sheaths bound in braided edges, cuffs anchored by carnelian — each one a quiet object of power meant to be lived in, not displayed in a case.

This is a brand built on devotion to craft and reverence for the natural world. It speaks softly, makes slowly, and treats every customer as family.

Brand essence

Earth-rooted, hand-forged adornment — where leather, stone, and intention become something worn.

In three words

Grounded. Mystical. Devoted.

02 — Brand Story

Made by hand, given with heart

Sage Holmes works under the name The Leather Sage from Asheville, North Carolina — a place where the Blue Ridge fog, the river, and the makers' culture all leave fingerprints on the work. Sage is both the maker and the muse: the same hands that cut and stitch the leather also model the pieces they create.

The craft is slow and personal. Commissions move through a custom list, one piece at a time, and each finished work is sent off with genuine gratitude. In Sage's own words about a favorite piece: "I've put so much love into this piece and I hope you feel it every time you wear it." That sentiment is the whole brand in a sentence — the belief that an object made with care carries that care forward.

The natural world is the constant source. Leaves, stone, bone, river-worn texture and the deep palette of the forest floor all find their way into the adornments. Nothing is mass-produced; everything is one of a kind.

"Creator of leather adornments inspired by the natural world."
03 — Positioning & Promise

Where the brand stands

Positioning statement

For free-spirited souls who adorn themselves with intention, The Leather Sage is a one-of-a-kind leather atelier that hand-sets gemstones into wearable leather art — because what you wear should be made by human hands, rooted in the earth, and meant to last.

The promise

Every piece is hand-cut, individually made, and finished with a maker's full attention. No two are alike. No piece leaves the studio without love in it.

What makes it different

  • Gemstone cabochons set directly into leather — a signature few makers attempt.
  • One maker, one pair of hands, start to finish.
  • Custom commission model — pieces built around the wearer.
  • Nature-drawn forms and an earthy, mystical sensibility.
  • A founder who both crafts and models the work, embodying the brand.

06 — Voice & Tone

How the brand speaks

The voice is warm, soulful, and grounded — a maker speaking directly and sincerely, never corporate or salesy. It is grateful, a little poetic, and reverent toward both craft and customer. It carries quiet mysticism without tipping into cliché.

Do

  • Speak in first person, as the maker.
  • Lead with gratitude and warmth.
  • Use earthy, sensory, natural imagery.
  • Honor the piece, the stone, and the wearer.
  • Be sincere and a touch poetic.

Don't

  • Sound like a big-box retailer.
  • Over-hype, discount, or pressure.
  • Use jargon or hollow buzzwords.
  • Lean on heavy mystical clichés.
  • Treat customers as transactions.

Voice in practice

"This one took its time. Cut by hand, braided at the edges, and set with a stone I'd been saving for the right piece. It's ready for whoever it's meant to find."
07 — Logo & Mark

The signature

The mark unites the two halves of the brand: a gemstone cabochon (the adornment, the meaning) cradled between a sage leaf and a leather frond (the natural world, the material), enclosed in a hand-drawn ring. It reads as a seal — a talisman in miniature.

THELEATHER SAGE

Primary lockup — mark above or beside the Cinzel wordmark.

Usage notes

  • Give the mark clear space equal to the height of the gemstone on all sides.
  • Reverse to bone/parchment on dark; use obsidian on light backgrounds.
  • The seal-ring mark may stand alone as an avatar, favicon, or stamp.
  • Never stretch, recolor the gemstone outside the palette, or add effects.

10 — Art Direction

How the work is shown

Photography

  • Natural light; warm, low, moody. Shadows welcome.
  • Texture-forward — show grain, stitch, stone, and patina up close.
  • Raw, earthy backdrops: weathered wood, stone, hide, foliage.
  • On-body shots that show the piece worn and alive.
  • Let the gemstone be the focal jewel of the frame.

Layout & feel

  • Dark canvas, generous negative space, slow pacing.
  • Brass hairline rules and seal motifs as quiet detail.
  • One hero image at a time — gallery, not grid-spam.
  • Type restrained; imagery does the talking.
  • Every screen should feel like the inside of the studio.
